Chicken Katsu Curry Recipe

Chicken Katsu Curry

Description

This is my favorite meal that I love to eat! If I don't make it myself, I'm usually eating it at Mr. Max restaurant in Dallas, TX. This dish is a savory, pank-crusted, chicken cutlet on top of a bed of white rice with Japanese curry sauce. Some people like to add pickled onions as garnish, or add carrots and potatoes into the curry mix. I like it either way!

Ingredients (serves one person or double ingredients for two)

  • Chicken Breast or Cutlet
  • Panko Bread Crumbs
  • 1/2 cup flower
  • 2 eggs
  • Japanese Curry Sauce/Cubes (I prefer Golden Brand)
  • 1 large carrot
  • 1 white potato
  • Vegetable oil

Cooking Steps!!!!!!!!!

  1. Pound flat the chicken breast and season with salt and pepper
  2. Whisk eggs in a bowl
  3. Put flower into a seperate bowl
  4. Put panko crumbs into a seperate bowl
  5. Dredge the chicken first in flour, then egg, then panko crumbs leaving no bald spots
  6. Fry the chicken breast in oil at 350ÂºF oil
  7. Chop potatoes and carrots into blocks/circles
  8. Sautee the potatoes and carrots in oil, and add curry blocks with 1/2 cup water. Stir until thick to your liking
  9. Once the chicken is done, set it off to the side
  10. Get your rice and put it in a dish
  11. Put your chicken on top of the rice
  12. Poor curry sauce on top
  13. Enjoy!
